Popular Chord Progressions and Their Songs

Posted on 2019-10-01 | Post modified: 2019-10-01 |

This post is going to talk about those popular chord progressions with songs as example. Once you know these popular chord progressions, you will amazingly find out that you can play so many songs without putting too much effort into it.

1. I - V - vi - IV

Songs that are written in this chord progression:

  1. Let it Be (verse part) - The Beatles
  2. Love Story (verse and chorus part) - Taylor Swift
  3. Hey Soul Sister (verse part) - Train
  4. Someone Like You (chorus part) - Adele

2. vi - IV - I - V

Beginning with a minor chord, this progression tends to sound darker or sadder than the other progression

Songs that use this chord progression:

  • Apologize - OneRepublic
  • Cheap Thrills - Sia
  • Faded (F# Major) - Alan Walker
  • Fade (F# Major) - Alan Walker
  • Always Remember Us This Way (C Major, verse: vi-IV-I-V, chorus: IV-I-vi-V) - Lady Gaga
  • River Flows in You (A Major) - Yiruma
  • The Scientist (F Major, verse: vi-IV-I-V, chorus: IV-IV-I-V) - Coldplay

See Quora - What are some popular songs with the chord progression: vi-IV-I-V (Am-F-C-G)?

3. The Famouse Canon Progression: I – V – vi – iii – IV – I – IV – V

This progression was initially found in Pachelbel’s Canon in D Major, a piece that was largely ignored after the composer’s death.
